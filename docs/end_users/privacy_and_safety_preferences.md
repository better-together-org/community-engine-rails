# Privacy and Safety Preferences

**Target Audience:** All community members  
**Document Type:** User Guide  
**Last Updated:** March 9, 2026

## Overview

Privacy and safety tools work together.

On the current platform, privacy-related safety choices appear in two main ways:

- privacy controls in some user-facing settings and content areas
- consent and safety preference choices inside the reporting flow

This page focuses on the controls you can use today and how to think about them safely.

## Privacy And Safety Are Not The Same Thing

Privacy settings control who can see some information.

Safety tools help you create distance, document harm, or ask for review.

In many situations, you may want both:

- a privacy change to reduce visibility
- a block or report to respond to harm

## Reporting Preferences You Can Choose Today

The current report form includes these safety-related choices:

### Consent To Contact

Use this when you want the safety team to be able to follow up with you.

If you leave this enabled:

- the platform may contact you for more information
- you may receive follow-up related to the report

### Consent To Restorative Process

Use this when you want the platform to know that restorative options may be considered if appropriate.

This does **not** guarantee that a restorative process will happen.

It also does **not** require you to participate in something unsafe.

### Retaliation Risk

Use this when you are worried that reporting, boundary-setting, or platform action could lead to escalation or retaliation.

This helps communicate urgency and context.

## Privacy Controls Elsewhere In The Platform

The broader platform also supports privacy controls for some content and profile visibility. Depending on the surface, you may be able to choose whether information is:

- public
- visible only to members
- visible only to community members
- private

Because the platform is still evolving, not every older doc page or settings note reflects the exact current UI state. When in doubt, use the visible controls in the interface and rely on the safety guides in this section for currently supported safety actions.

## Choosing The Right Tool

### Use a privacy change when:

- you want fewer people to see something
- the issue is visibility, not direct harm

### Use blocking when:

- you want distance from a specific person

### Use reporting when:

- you want the platform to review harmful behavior, unsafe content, or a pattern of harm
