# Emergency and Urgent Situations

**Target Audience:** All community members  
**Document Type:** User Guide  
**Last Updated:** March 9, 2026

## Overview

If you or someone else is in immediate danger, the platform reporting form is not the first step.

Use emergency and crisis resources first.

## If There Is Immediate Danger

1. Contact local emergency services.
2. Move to a safer place if possible.
3. Reach out to trusted local support if you can.
4. Use the platform later to document what happened or request follow-up.

## If The Situation Is Urgent But Not An Emergency

Use the platform report form as soon as you safely can.

When you report:

- choose the harm level that best reflects urgency
- use the retaliation-risk field if escalation is a concern
- use blocking if you need immediate distance from a person

## Preserve Information Safely

If it is safe to do so, keep your own record of:

- screenshots
- links
- dates and times
- a short note about what happened

You do not need perfect evidence to report harm, but your own notes can still help you later.

## What The Platform Can Do Afterward

The platform may help with:

- documenting the report
- reviewing content or behavior
- follow-up contact, if you allowed it
- internal moderation or safety review

The platform is **not** a substitute for:

- emergency services
- medical help
- crisis lines
- legal representation

## If You Are Not Sure Whether It Counts As “Urgent”

You can still report.

If something feels unsafe, escalating, or likely to continue, it is appropriate to document it and ask for support.
