# Reporting Harm and Safety Concerns

**Target Audience:** All community members  
**Document Type:** User Guide  
**Last Updated:** March 9, 2026

## Overview

Use the reporting flow when you want the platform to review harmful behavior, unsafe interactions, or content that should be checked by the safety team.

The current reporting interface supports a structured report with:

- category
- harm level
- requested outcome
- short summary
- private details
- consent and retaliation-risk choices

## Where Reporting Starts

In the current interface, report links are available from:

- person profile pages
- post pages

The reporting system can support additional reportable types internally, but user-facing entry points currently focus on profiles and posts.

## What The Report Form Asks

### Category

Choose the option that best matches the concern.

Examples:

- harassment
- hate speech
- discrimination
- spam or scam
- privacy violation
- boundary violation

### Harm Level

This helps the safety team understand urgency.

General guide:

- **Low**: uncomfortable or concerning, but not urgent
- **Medium**: meaningful harm or repeated concern
- **High**: serious harm that needs prompt review
- **Urgent**: immediate safety concern that should be triaged as quickly as possible

### Requested Outcome

This tells the platform what kind of support you want.

Examples:

- boundary support
- mediated conversation
- community accountability
- content review
- temporary protection

You do not need to know exactly which outcome is right before reporting. Use the closest match.

### Short Summary

Use this for the clearest short description of what happened.

### Private Details For The Safety Team

Use this for supporting context that may help with review, such as:

- pattern of behavior
- dates or timing
- specific phrases
- evidence you want the reviewer to know

### Safety Preferences

The report form also asks:

- whether the platform may contact you
- whether restorative options may be considered if appropriate
- whether you are worried about retaliation or escalation

## Step-By-Step: How To Submit A Report

1. Open a profile or post that has a **Report** action.
2. Select **Report**.
3. Complete the report form.
4. Review your answers.
5. Submit the report.

## Writing A Helpful Report

The most helpful reports are:

- specific
- factual
- focused on the behavior or content
- clear about what support you need

Helpful example:

> This person contacted me repeatedly after I asked them to stop. I am asking for boundary support and I am worried about retaliation.

Less helpful example:

> They are the worst.

## What To Include

Include:

- what happened
- who was involved
- what made it harmful or unsafe
- whether this has happened before
- whether you want follow-up contact

You can also preserve your own evidence, such as screenshots or notes, in case you need it later.

## What Reporting Does Not Replace

Reporting in the platform does **not** replace:

- emergency services
- medical care
- legal advice
- crisis response

If the situation is urgent or dangerous, read [Emergency and Urgent Situations](emergency_and_urgent_situations.md) first.
