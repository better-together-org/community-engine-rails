# Reporting Harm and Safety Concerns

**Target Audience:** All community members  
**Document Type:** User Guide  
**Last Updated:** April 8, 2026

## Overview

Use the reporting flow when you want the platform to review harmful behavior, unsafe interactions, or content that should be checked by the safety team.

The current reporting interface supports a structured report with:

- category
- harm level
- requested outcome
- short summary
- private details
- consent and retaliation-risk choices

## Where Reporting Starts

In the current interface, reporting starts from a shared **feedback area** on supported content and profile surfaces.

Current entry points include:

- person profile pages
- post pages
- page views
- individual reportable page sections or content blocks
- community pages
- event pages
- conversation messages
- upload listings when a reportable upload is visible

The same entry pattern is used so you do not need to learn a different reporting location for each surface. The label changes to match scope, such as **Page feedback**, **Post feedback**, **Community feedback**, or **Section feedback**.

When you choose **Report safety issue**, the report goes to the **platform safety team**. It is not automatically shared with the content owner, profile owner, or community/page stewards.

## What The Report Form Asks

### Category

Choose the option that best matches the concern.

Examples:

- harassment
- hate speech
- discrimination
- spam or scam
- privacy violation
- boundary violation

### Harm Level

This helps the safety team understand urgency.

General guide:

- **Low**: uncomfortable or concerning, but not urgent
- **Medium**: meaningful harm or repeated concern
- **High**: serious harm that needs prompt review
- **Urgent**: immediate safety concern that should be triaged as quickly as possible

### Requested Outcome

This tells the platform what kind of support you want.

Examples:

- boundary support
- mediated conversation
- community accountability
- content review
- temporary protection

You do not need to know exactly which outcome is right before reporting. Use the closest match.

### Short Summary

Use this for the clearest short description of what happened.

### Private Details For The Safety Team

Use this for supporting context that may help with review, such as:

- pattern of behavior
- dates or timing
- specific phrases
- evidence you want the reviewer to know

### Safety Preferences

The report form also asks:

- whether the platform may contact you
- whether restorative options may be considered if appropriate
- whether you are worried about retaliation or escalation

## Who Can See And Act On A Safety Report

For the current reporting flow:

- the report is sent privately to the platform safety team
- the people who can review and act on it are platform safety reviewers
- after submission, it stays visible to you and platform safety reviewers
- content owners and stewards do not automatically receive the report

This keeps safety disclosures private. Future non-safety feedback, such as correction suggestions or contribution ideas, will use a separate steward-routed workflow.

## Step-By-Step: How To Submit A Report

1. Open the person, post, page, page section, community, event, message, or upload you need to flag.
2. Find the visible feedback area for that surface.
3. Select **Report safety issue**.
4. Complete the report form.
5. Review your answers.
6. Submit the report.

## Writing A Helpful Report

The most helpful reports are:

- specific
- factual
- focused on the behavior or content
- clear about what support you need

Helpful example:

> This person contacted me repeatedly after I asked them to stop. I am asking for boundary support and I am worried about retaliation.

Less helpful example:

> They are the worst.

## What To Include

Include:

- what happened
- who was involved
- what made it harmful or unsafe
- whether this has happened before
- whether you want follow-up contact

You can also preserve your own evidence, such as screenshots or notes, in case you need it later.

## After You Submit

After submission, the report detail page remains the main place to:

- confirm what record you reported
- review your submitted information
- see participant-visible follow-up notes
- add additional evidence or an appeal note if the situation changes

## What Reporting Does Not Replace

Reporting in the platform does **not** replace:

- emergency services
- medical care
- legal advice
- crisis response

If the situation is urgent or dangerous, read [Emergency and Urgent Situations](emergency_and_urgent_situations.md) first.
